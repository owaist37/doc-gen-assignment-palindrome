## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

In light of the inheritance you received and your current investment strategy, we recommend pooling your available funds to maximise your investment in your Individual Savings Accounts (ISAs) and to establish a new Joint Investment Account for any remaining balance.

First, let’s outline the sources of funds:
- The inheritance from Jean’s late mother amounts to £120,000.
- The joint General Investment Account (GIA) currently has a value of £30,000, as per the most recent account information.

This gives us a total available pool of:
- £120,000 (inheritance) + £30,000 (joint GIA) = **£150,000**.

Next, we will allocate this total to your ISAs and then to the new joint investment account:
- Robert has an ISA headroom of £20,000, which we will fully utilise.
- Jean also has an ISA headroom of £20,000, which we will fully utilise as well.

Thus, the ISA contributions total:
- £20,000 (Robert's ISA) + £20,000 (Jean's ISA) = **£40,000**.

After making these contributions, the remaining balance to be allocated to the new joint investment account is:
- £150,000 (total pool) - £40,000 (ISA contributions) = **£110,000**.

In summary, we recommend that you contribute £20,000 each to Robert's and Jean's ISAs and allocate the remaining £110,000 to the new joint investment account, ensuring your investments align with your long-term growth objectives. Please note that the initial charge for the new account will be 0.5%.
