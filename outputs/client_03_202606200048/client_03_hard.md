## Background & Objectives

In our recent review meeting, we acknowledged the difficult circumstances surrounding Jean's mother's passing earlier this spring. Jean is coping as best as she can, and we discussed how she has received an inheritance of approximately £120,000 from her late mother's estate. Both Jean and Robert expressed their desire to invest this new money alongside their existing portfolio, with a focus on long-term growth. They have made it clear that, for the foreseeable future, they do not require any income from their investments. Their risk profile remains at a moderate level, and they are comfortable with maintaining this approach. We also considered their family goals, including plans to support their grandchildren and a trip later in the year, which do not change our investment recommendations. Overall, our aim is to put the inheritance to work effectively, aligning it with their investment strategy while being sensitive to the context of its origin.

## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

We recommend that you proceed with a disinvestment from your joint General Investment Account (GIA) with a total value of £30,000. This will provide liquidity to help fund top-ups to your ISAs and a new joint investment account. 

You have received an inheritance of £120,000, which we will also allocate towards your investments. Therefore, the total funds available for investment will be £30,000 from the GIA and £120,000 from the inheritance, giving a combined total of £150,000.

We propose the following allocation of these funds:

1. **ISA Top-ups**:
   - Top-up Robert's Stocks & Shares ISA (account ID: H-ISA-R) with £70,000.
   - Top-up Jean's Stocks & Shares ISA (account ID: H-ISA-JE) with £66,000.

2. **New Joint Investment Account**:
   - Establish a new joint investment account with the remaining balance of £14,000.

Please note that ongoing platform charges will apply to your investments. The initial charge for setting up the new joint account will be 0.5% of the investment amount. We will confirm the exact nature of these charges in the relevant section of this report.
