## Background & Objectives

This report covers the Holloway ISAs held by Robert and Jean Fletcher, in addition to their jointly-held General Investment Account and a new joint account we plan to establish. Following our recent discussions, it is clear that both clients remain focused on long-term growth, with no immediate need for income from their investments. Their risk profile is moderate, which aligns with their preference for a balanced approach to investment.

Jean, having recently received an inheritance following her mother's passing, wishes to allocate these funds strategically alongside their existing portfolio. This new capital will complement their ongoing investment strategy, allowing us to enhance both of their ISAs to maximise tax efficiency while also setting up a joint investment account to manage any surplus funds effectively.

Overall, our primary objective remains to position their investments for growth over the long term, taking into account their family considerations and future aspirations.

## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

We will begin by pooling all the available sources of funds for immediate investment. The total inheritance received by Jean from her late mother’s estate is £120,000. Additionally, we will consider the current value of the jointly-held General Investment Account (GIA), which is £30,000 as recorded in the client data. Therefore, our net investable total is calculated as follows:

- Inheritance: £120,000
- Joint GIA value: £30,000

This totals £150,000 available for investment. There are no committed repayments or loan repayments indicated in the source documents, so the entire amount will be considered for allocation.

Next, we will allocate this pooled amount to the available investment options while respecting the ISA headroom. Each account can receive contributions as follows:

- Robert Fletcher’s ISA (H-ISA-R): £20,000 (full headroom)
- Jean Fletcher’s ISA (H-ISA-JE): £20,000 (full headroom)

After allocating these amounts, the contributions made to the ISAs will total £40,000 (£20,000 + £20,000), leaving a balance of £110,000 from our net investable total.

The next step involves allocating the remaining funds to the joint GIA, as indicated in the report request. We will disinvest the entire value of the joint GIA, which is £30,000. As a result, our remaining balance after these allocations will be as follows:

- Total funds after ISA contributions: £110,000
- Less joint GIA value: £30,000

This provides us with £80,000 remaining to allocate towards the new jointly-held investment account.

In conclusion, we will make full contributions of £20,000 into each of Robert's and Jean's ISAs while disinvesting the entire £30,000 from the joint GIA. The remaining funds will be allocated to the new joint investment account, with the remaining balance to be confirmed once the specific amount for this account is finalised. Hence, the allocation is as follows:

- Robert's ISA (H-ISA-R): £20,000
- Jean's ISA (H-ISA-JE): £20,000
- Joint GIA: £30,000 (disinvested)
- New joint investment account: £80,000, with the remaining balance to be confirmed upon finalisation.
