## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

In light of the recent inheritance of £120,000 from Jean's late mother's estate and the current value of the joint General Investment Account (GIA) at £38,000, we recommend taking the following actions to align with your long-term growth objectives while maintaining your moderate risk profile.

Firstly, we advise disinvesting the entire joint GIA of £38,000. This amount will be combined with the inherited funds to fund top-ups for both your Stocks & Shares ISAs. We suggest contributing £60,000 to Robert's ISA and £60,000 to Jean's ISA, making full use of your annual allowance for the new tax year.

The total investment, therefore, will amount to £120,000 from the inheritance and the £38,000 from the GIA for the ISAs. After the ISA top-ups, any remaining balance from the joint GIA of approximately £10,000 will be allocated to open a new jointly-held investment account.

Be mindful of the platform's initial charge of 0.5% that will apply to the new joint account when it is established. This charge will ensure that your investments are appropriately managed according to your collective goals. We believe this strategy will allow you both to make the most of your enhanced investment position while remaining consistent with your risk tolerance and investment objectives.
