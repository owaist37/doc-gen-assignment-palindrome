## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

In our recent review meeting, we discussed your jointly-held General Investment Account (GIA) and the potential for simplifying your investment structure. Given that both of you are now retired and do not require any immediate income from your investments, we believe that it would be advantageous to disinvest the full amount from your joint GIA, which is currently valued at approximately £40,000.

We recommend that the proceeds from the GIA sale be used to top up both of your Stocks & Shares ISAs. This strategy allows you to take advantage of the tax-efficient growth potential offered by ISAs. By splitting the funds equally, David’s and Susan’s ISAs will each receive a boost, enhancing your overall investment position while maximising your annual tax wrapper allowances for the new tax year.

Keeping the ISAs allows for a balanced approach to risk that we have agreed upon in past discussions. As the equity markets have shown resilience lately, we are optimistic about the growth potential of the investments held within these accounts. Additionally, this move aligns well with your objective of maintaining a simplified investment strategy.

Please let us know if you are comfortable with this approach, and we will prepare the necessary actions to facilitate this change.

## Tax Implications

Please be aware that the disposal of the joint General Investment Account may give rise to a capital gains tax liability. Any gain arising will be assessed against the annual exempt amount.
