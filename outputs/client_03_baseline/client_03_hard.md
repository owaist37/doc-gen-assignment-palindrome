## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

In light of the recent inheritance of £120,000 that has come from Jean's late mother's estate, we recommend using this new capital to enhance both your investment strategy and tax efficiency. Our approach involves funding both your Stocks & Shares ISAs to the maximum contribution limit for the upcoming tax year, as well as disinvesting your existing joint General Investment Account (GIA) that currently holds approximately £38,000.

Here is our proposed action plan:

1. **Top-Up Your ISAs**: We suggest maximising the contributions to both your ISA accounts. This is a beneficial strategy as it allows you to invest tax-efficiently, with any capital gains or income generated within the ISAs remaining tax-free.

2. **Disinvest from the Joint GIA**: We will proceed with selling the holdings within the joint GIA. This is necessary to realise the current value, which you have noted increased since your last statement, and to facilitate the reinvestment into your ISAs and the new joint investment account. 

3. **Open a New Joint Investment Account**: After topping up your ISAs, any remaining balance from the proceeds of the GIA and the inheritance can be allocated to a new joint investment account. This account will allow you to maintain a diversified investment portfolio while keeping in line with your risk profile.

It is important to note that selling assets in the joint GIA may create a capital gains tax liability. However, we will ensure that we are mindful of your annual exempt amount to minimise any potential tax impacts.
