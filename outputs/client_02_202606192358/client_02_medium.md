## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

We recommend that David and Susan proceed to disinvest the full value of their jointly-held General Investment Account (GIA) currently valued at £40,000. The proceeds from this account will be used to top up both David's and Susan's Stocks & Shares ISAs for the new tax year, split equally between the two ISAs.

This approach will simplify their investment strategy by consolidating their assets into their ISAs, which offer tax advantages that the GIA does not. By topping up their ISAs, they will maximise their tax-efficient investment allowance, ensuring that their funds are working harder for them in a sheltered environment. Both ISAs are already part-funded for the year, and using the GIA to top up will fully utilise their remaining allowances without impacting their income requirements, as neither client requires any income from the portfolio at this time.

Additionally, we will review the ongoing charges associated with this change and provide clarity on how these will affect their overall investment costs. It is essential to consider that disinvesting from the GIA may incur a capital gains tax liability; we will outline the details of this potential liability in the Tax Implications section of the report.
