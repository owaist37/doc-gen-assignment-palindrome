## Recommendations

We recommend the following:

Based on our recent discussions and your objectives, we recommend taking the following steps with your investments. Firstly, we will disinvest the entire holding in your joint General Investment Account (GIA), which currently has an approximate value of £30,000. This action will allow us to utilise these funds alongside the inheritance you received, amounting to £120,000, for further investment.

We propose to top up both your Stocks and Shares ISAs with the total amount from the inheritance and the joint GIA funds. By contributing to your ISAs, we can ensure the growth of your investments is tax-efficient, as ISAs provide protection from income and capital gains tax.

After topping up both ISAs, any remaining balance will be allocated to establish a new jointly-held investment account. This new account will allow you to invest in a diversified portfolio in alignment with your moderate risk profile.

It is important to note that selling your joint GIA may result in a capital gains tax liability, which will be assessed against the annual exempt amount. We will ensure that this is clearly communicated, and the implications are taken into consideration moving forward.

This strategy aligns with your long-term growth objectives, as you have indicated that you do not require income from the portfolio for the foreseeable future. By following this plan, we aim to enhance your investment potential while managing the associated risks effectively.

## Tax Implications

Please be aware that the disposal of the joint General Investment Account may give rise to a capital gains tax liability. Any gain arising will be assessed against the annual exempt amount.
